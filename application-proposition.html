Application Proposition


To enable us to help you in realize your dreamz, we employ three distinct models that helps us in applying to the right mix of universities for you. These models, in consultation with us, help you in crafting an application strategy that would help you cast your net judiciously. Our models – the “top-spin” model, the “firm-base” model and the “sure-shot” model are all designed to help the students maximize their opportunities as well as prevent them from shooting in the dark or wasting precious resources like efforts, time and money.

The Top-Spin Model:

The 'top-spin' model substantiates the efforts of our firm in helping students to apply to more 'ambitious' universities and few universities that belong to the 'fit' or 'safe' universities category. The 'top-spin' model helps ambitious students to aggressively knock at universities where their profile competes with bigger/better profile in the pool. This approach also ensures that minimal efforts and money is spent on universities that are considered 'safe'.

The Firm-Base Model:

The 'firm-base' model is preferred by students who would like to balance their options, between 'ambitious' universities, 'fit' universities and 'safe' universities, and is also the most preferred model for college fresher's or senior students. This model helps the students to apply to all the 3 categories on the same weightage, and thus explore their options without bias. This model also helps the student to be ambitious and also hunt for aid opportunities where possible.

The Sure-Shot Model:

The 'sure-shot' model is for those who are either 'aid-conscious' or for those who would want to play it safe. In this model high emphasis is laid on applying to universities wherein the applicant has higher chance of securing admits and or aid wherever possible and much emphasis is on applying to universities that are either 'safe' or 'fit'. As the model is loaded heavily at the bottom, the applicant maximizes his chances of admits/aid and thus achieves his objective.
